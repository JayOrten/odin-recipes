Milk Balls (Gulab Jamun)

These are fried balls made of sweetened milk. Think condensed milk bombs!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 piece cinnamon stick, or 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 2 drops lemon juice, Optional
  • 1/2 cup powdered milk
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh milk
  • oil, for frying

Steps

  1. Place sugar, cinnamon stick, water and lemon juice in a deep pot or saucepan, and heat over a medium flame.

    Bring to a boil, and then lower heat until it's at a simmer. Cook until it reaches the thread stage or turns little sticky while stirring occasionally.

    (It will take around 8-10 minutes over medium flame to reach the required consistency.) When the sugar syrup is ready, turn off the flame. Keep warm.

  2. Place milk powder, flour and baking soda in a large bowl.

    Add oil, and mix well. Sprinkle fresh milk evenly over the mixture and gently mix it.

  3. Grease your palms with oil and divide mixture into marble-sized small portions (around 9-10).

    Take each portion and form solid-looking balls. (Make sure each ball doesn't have any cracks.) Set aside.

  4. Heat enough oil for deep frying in a saucepan or pot over medium heat. Slowly drop 3 to 4 balls into the hot oil, and reduce the heat to low.

    Deep fry until the balls turn a golden brown, around 6 to 7 minutes.

    Transfer each ball using a slotted spoon to paper towels to absorb excess oil. Repeat with remaining balls.

  5. Heat the sugar syrup for 3 to 4 minutes, and then add the milk balls.

    Keep the balls in the syrup for at least 2 hours before serving so the balls absorb the syrup. (Balls will increase in size as the syrup is absorbed.) Serve at room temperature.
